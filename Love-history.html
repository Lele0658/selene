Love History:

Selene's most famous love story is with the mortal Endymion. According to the myth, Selene fell deeply in love with him and would visit him every night while he slept in a cave on Mount Latmus. In some versions of the myth, Selene asked Zeus to grant Endymion eternal sleep so that he would remain forever young and beautiful. This way, she could admire him for eternity without fear of losing him to age or death.

Whatever his profession, the handsome young man frequently slept beneath the open sky. Selene saw him as she passed overhead and fell deeply in love. Selene was so enamored of the young man that she left the sky to see him more closely. She walked as quietly as possible to avoid frightening Endymion as he slept. She began to do this every night, leaving her chariot behind and making the sky dark with her absence. Occasionally she asked her brother to take over her duties so she could spend more time on the ground with Endymion.

Selene and Endymion had several children together, including fifty daughters known as the Menae, who represented the lunar months. This union symbolized the connection between the moon and the passage of time.
