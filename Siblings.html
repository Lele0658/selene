Selene's Siblings:

Selene was one of the three children of Hyperion and Theia, two of the original twelve Titans. Her father was the Titan god of heavenly light, while her mother represented the blue color of the sky. Together, they fittingly created three sources of celestial illumination. Their daughter Eos was the first to appear each day. She was the goddess of the dawn and brought new light to the world every morning.

Helios, the second child, was the god of the sun. He rode his golden chariot across the sky each day, illuminating the earth with his bright rays. Selene, as the moon goddess, followed in her brother's footsteps, but her chariot was silver and drawn by two snow-white horses. This imagery symbolized the cool, reflective light of the moon as opposed to the bright, direct light of the sun.

Eos, the goddess of dawn, is one of the most fascinating figures in Greek mythology. She embodied the first light of day and was often depicted as a youthful winged figure bringing the dawn. Her role symbolized not just the beginning of a new day but also the transformative power of light and renewal.

The three siblings represented the celestial bodies that govern the day and night, each playing a vital role in the natural order of the world. Their combined presence in the sky created a harmonious balance between light and darkness, day and night.
